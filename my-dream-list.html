My Dream List (Ravens Edition)

These are the Ravens cards I want long-term — legends + modern cornerstones. Values are rough ranges depending on grade, parallels, autos, and market swings. Although if I was to be honest with you I really enjoy ripping packs of cards, this dream list might remain unfullfilled for some time.

Ray Lewis — 1996 Topps Chrome Rookie

Franchise icon. The must-own Ravens card.

Rough value: $300 – $1,500+

Ed Reed — 2002 Bowman Chrome Rookie

All-time great safety. Pick-six legend.

Rough value: $150 – $800+

Lamar Jackson — 2018 Prizm Rookie

Modern centerpiece for the franchise.

Rough value: $50 – $1,000+

Joe Flacco — 2008 Topps Chrome Rookie

Super Bowl MVP. Rings matter.

Rough value: $40 – $250+

Terrell Suggs — 2003 Topps Chrome Rookie

Peak Ravens defensive identity.

Rough value: $50 – $300+

Jonathan Ogden — 1996 Bowman’s Best Rookie

First Ravens draft pick ever. Hall of Fame tackle.

Rough value: $100 – $600+

Zay Flowers — Rookie Card

Modern WR upside. Strong PC piece.

Rough value: $20 – $150+

Kyle Hamilton — Rookie Card

Defense cornerstone potential.

Rough value: $15 – $120+

Roquan Smith — Autograph

Leader of the defense. Autos preferred.

Rough value: $40 – $200+

Mark Andrews — Rookie Autograph

TE legend loading. Rookie auto staple.

Rough value: $50 – $250+

Priority Order

First: Ray / Ed / Lamar centerpiece cards.
Second: Andrews auto + Roquan auto.
Then modern rookies once the right parallel shows up.
